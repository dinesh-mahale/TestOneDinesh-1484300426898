A one day ACIT Design Thinking workshop brought together a multi-disciplinary team from Accenture to define a quick approach to NBN information seeking process on FTTX deployment metrics


Design thinking workshop helped participants empathize with the project's persona: Benoit, an NBN manager. Empathizing with the Persona led the team to identifying the several pain points reflected on feelings during the process of getting information : Frustration, Confusion and time consuming.


After ideating ways to solve Benoit's needs, the team established the project vision that will give direction to a product design: "As an nbn manager. Benoit can interact with virtual assistant to receive accurate answers in few seconds to questions about data, metrics and analyses. The experience would be similar to that of interacting with human analyst within his team."

The team agreed on a hypothesis that will help to test success metrics:
-Quicker access to information
-Consistency of information provided


At the end of the session the team defines future actions to help provide more clarity on what is in/out of scope on a minimal viable product (MVP).

Goals and Non-Goal

Assumptions and Risks

Currently, the NBN users get access to FTTX deployment metrics via an email sent to the analyst. Currently, a significant amount of time and effort is spent by analysts and SMEs in catering to the information requests of the winder organisation. Replacing the existing system with a Virtual Assistant, would act as a single source of truth for the organisation for metrics and related information about the progress of FTTX. Misalignment in number and information can be avoided. Duplicate of reporting can be avoided






  1. Capture usage statistics
  2. Text interaction
  3. I can provide feedback on the quality of response to the assistant
  4. To be able to do basic queries(e.g.: How many premises were connected last week? ) in given dataset. Numeric answer should be returned with clarification text (E.g.:There were 30000 premises connected(Reached RFS) last week.)
  5. To be able to provide definitions (E.g.: What is the definition of metric M15?)
  6. To be able to do basic comparison queries (e.g.: How has the cycle time of process ABC improved over last month?; Virtual Assistant should return an answer such as Cycle time of process ABC reduced from 30 days to 25 days)
  7. A mechanism to alert the user that information they are accessing is confidential or sensitive.
  8. A response within a matter of seconds.
  9. It should be able to follow on the context of a question from the previos question: similar to an open conversation (E.g : User: How many SAMs completed construction last week? Virtual Assistant: 0 SAMs completed construction last week. User:What about this week? Virtual Assistant : 5 SAMs completed construction this week)
  10. If the virtual assistant cannot provide the answer, it should refer the user to a specific person or other report.
  11. Virtual Assistant is able to ask clarification questions.
  12. Stand-alone Login page to stimulate the experience of a user signing in from the internet.
  13. Virtual Assistant will appear as a pop-up that can be made full screen
  14. English Language
  15. All questions and responses should be able to asked/received in natural language(with nbn-specific jargon)
  16. Audit and Loggin.
  1. Voice- asking questions and returning answers.
  2. Returning visualization within the interface.
  3. The virtual assistant understands my context as a user before I ask question based on organisational details associated with my account.
  4. Single Sign-on for nbn users.
  5. Auto fill features while typing in the Chat window in English or using NBN specific words, spell check.
  • Textual in interaction with the Bot
  • Provide feedback on the quality of the response

  • Capable of providing definitions for nbn jargons
  • Capable of answering basic comparison questions
  • Provide disclaimer on the sensitive information
  • Quick response
  • Able to understand conversation context
  • Refer user to an existing report or analyst
  • Bot is able to ask clarification questions to the user
  • Login to Bot
  • Chat appears as a pop up and goes full screen
